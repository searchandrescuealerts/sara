About

Search and Rescue Alerts (SARA) is a new idea that puts “eyes on the group” almost instantaneously when a person is missing. The system used by Law Enforcement Agencies now is based on creating a poster, notifying officers, notifying the media, and getting people to look. On average, it takes 2-3 hours to get the word out when a person is reported missing.

But by opting in to this service, the Law Enforcement Agencies can contact you directly and instantly when a person is reported missing. Within 5 minutes there are 100’s of people aware of the missing person. You can look for the missing person as you continue through you normal activities of the day, like driving kids to school, shopping, or visiting parks.

Also, when a person goes missing often search parties are organized. But in order to participate in a search party one must be approved. This approval process involves providing basic personal information so a simple background check can be done, and occasionally watching a short training video. Doing these administrative tasks before the moment of the emergency can help spend more time on the search.
